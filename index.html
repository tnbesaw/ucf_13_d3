Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income


Household Income (Median) has strong correlations amongst all three health risks:

  • Obesity is more prevalent to those with a lower household income.
  • Smokers are more prevalent to those with a lower household income.
  • Lacking Healthcare is more common to those with a lower household income.

Interesting outliers:

  • The "deep south" has a significantly rate of obesity vs. the rest of the country. Four of the five states with the highest percentage of obesity are from this region (AR, MS, LA, AL)
  • Massachusetts by far has the lowest percentage of people lacking healthcare at just 4.6%.
  • Texas, conversely, has the highest percentage of people hacking healthcare at an astounding 24.9%
  • Utah has significantly lower percentage of smokers compared to the rest of the country at just 9.7%.
